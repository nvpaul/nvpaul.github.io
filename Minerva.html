Define

The life expectancy (LE) and health-adjusted life expectancy (HALE) in Canada have been increasing consistently over the past decade, however, two-thirds of Canadian adults live with at least one modifiable risk factor for chronic disease, including:

  • Tobacco smoking
  • Physical inactivity
  • Sedentary behaviour
  • Unhealthy eating
  • Harmful use of alcohol
  • Obesity

In fact, Canada ranks among the worst of Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) countries for adult obesity rates. Although there has been a major decrease in mortality in Canada, the overall burden of disease is increasing.

Canadians are living longer, but it would be best if we lived longer in good health.

Some of Canada's reported areas of concern stem from the following stats:
1 in 6 Canadians are 65 years or older, with this population growing 4 times faster than the overall population.
1 in 5 Canadian adults live with a chronic disease.
2 in 3 Canadian adult deaths are caused by major chronic diseases.
2 in 3 Canadian adults live with a modifiable risk factor for chronic disease.

These risk factors that place the health of Canadian adults in danger are modifiable. With individual, community, and population interventions, we could help lower these stats and help Canadians live healthier lives. Minerva strives to accomplish this task through individual education.


[1] Public Health Agency of Canada, “How Healthy are Canadians?,” Government of Canada, 11-Apr-2017.
[2] OECD, “Obesity Update,” Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development, 2018.
[3] World Health Organization, 2013. Global Action Plan for the Prevention and Control of Noncommunicable Diseases 2013-2020.

Ideate

Idea 1:
Voice Platform Health Hack of the Day

How it Works
This voice skill on Amazon Alexa, "Health Hack of the Day," would be similar to asking for a fun fact; however, the resulting answer would be a short piece of advice realted to improving your health.

Pros

  • Voice is a new technology platform that is rapidly growing in popularity
  • Provides information to users hands-free

Cons

  • No room for additional details, the fact must be short and simple
  • Ensure the fact is randomized among a large dataset, preventing repeated information

Idea 2:
Social Media Style Health Hack Sharing Website

How it Works
A social media style feed would display information to users, allow users to save favourites, and provide links to additional resources.

Pros

  • Allows for a short and simple message with a link to more information
  • Users can save and return to favourtie tips
  • Social media is growing in popularity, and thus would be easy to navigate

Cons

  • Without a review of the information, there may be fake information circulated

Idea 3:
Smart Watch Fitness Tracker

How it Works
The increasingly popular fitness trackers could be connected to a users phone through a mobile app. This could leverage the health data collected by the tracker and provide rewards for meeting specific goals.

Pros

  • Rewards offer extra encouragement for users to meet health goals
  • Gamify the experience

Cons

  • Depending on the type of reward offered, there is an additional cost
